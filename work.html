Work.

My entire life I thought that people who can code have some sort of a superhero power. It fascinated me how someone was able to create things to make other people’s life easier. When I was 30 years and 11 months old I started looking into it myself (Okay, also there was my friend who was constantly telling me that I’m going to like it), I found out either it’s not that complicated or I’m not as hopeless as I thought.

Before that beautiful coding enlightenment happened in my life I was busy with other things. Like when I lived in Russia I gained a Master’s degree in Public relations, for 5 years worked as a PR manager in various fields most of the time media related. After recent crisis (because of annexation of Crimea) I was made redundant and there was no career opportunities for me I decided to change my career path and a country. I became a certified English teacher and came to China. I’ve been in China for three years and here my beautiful coding enlightenment took place. (You can find my detailed CV on LinkedIn or just request it from me).

When I was diagnosed with love for coding - first, I talked to people, second, I started teaching myself html, css and JavaScript basics (thanks to SoloLearn, edX), third, I started going to different tech orientated events in town (Apparently, Shanghai has a lot). Finally, I decided to built this website to understand if I love coding as it seemed to me at the beginning.

Now I am sure that I really like website development.
